Carbonara

Carbonara is made with guanciale (cured pork), eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, spaghetti pasta, and lots of black pepper. Italians don't add extra ingredients like cream, milk, garlic, or onions. Try this recipe if you want to make an authentic, creamy carbonara that comes straight from Italy, where I live. Buon appetito!

Ingredients:

  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 4 ounces guanciale (cured pork jowl), pancetta (Italian bacon), or bacon, cut into 1/4-inch dice
  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup reserved pasta water

    • And that is everything. Put it all together, real nice, and you've got a carbonara.

Here's how:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat; add guanciale (see Cook's Note). Cook, turning occasionally, until evenly browned and crispy, 5 to 10 minutes. Remove from heat and drain on paper towels.
  2. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring occasionally until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes. Drain, reserving 1/4 cup of the pasta water.
  3. Whisk eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pepper, and salt together in a bowl.
  4. Combine spaghetti, guanciale, and egg mixture in a large bowl. Add reserved pasta water, 1 tablespoon at a time, until sauce is creamy and clings to the pasta.
  5. Sprinkle with parsley and serve immediately.
